Quick map:

What each symbol is

Broad equity & style ETFs:
SPY – SPDR S&P 500 ETF, tracks the S&P 500 (large-cap US stocks).   SSGA
QQQ – Invesco QQQ Trust, tracks the Nasdaq-100 (100 largest non-financials on Nasdaq, very tech heavy).  Invesco
IWM – iShares Russell 2000 ETF, tracks the Russell 2000 (US small caps).  BlackRock
EFA – iShares MSCI EAFE ETF, developed markets ex-US & Canada (Europe, Australasia, Far East).
EEM – iShares MSCI Emerging Markets ETF, broad emerging-market equities.


Bond / rates ETFs
TLT – iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF (long-duration US Treasuries).
IEF – iShares 7–10 Year Treasury Bond ETF (intermediate US Treasuries).
HYG – iShares iBoxx High Yield Corporate Bond ETF (US high-yield / junk bonds).LQD – iShares iBoxx Investment Grade Corporate Bond ETF (US IG corporate bonds).

Real assets & commodities:
GLD – SPDR Gold Shares (physical gold).
USO – United States Oil Fund (front-month WTI crude oil futures).
VNQ – Vanguard Real Estate ETF (US equity REITs).
DBC – Invesco DB Commodity Index Tracking Fund (broad commodity futures basket).

S&P 500 Sector SPDRs (each is a sector slice of the S&P 500):
XLK – Technology
XLF – Financials
XLE – Energy
XLU – Utilities
XLY – Consumer Discretionary
XLI – Industrials
XLB – Materials
XLP – Consumer Staples

So your BASE_UNIVERSE is a very standard multi-asset ETF universe for tactical allocation / timing models.
